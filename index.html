Car Loan EMI Calculator FAQ

What is EMI in car loans?
EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It's the fixed amount you pay monthly to repay your car loan, which includes both principal and interest components.
How is car loan EMI calculated?
EMI is calculated using the formula: EMI = [P x R x (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N - 1], where P is principal, R is monthly interest rate, and N is the number of monthly installments.
Can I prepay my car loan to reduce EMI burden?
Yes, most lenders allow prepayment. You can either reduce your EMI amount while keeping the same tenure, or reduce the tenure while maintaining the same EMI amount.
What factors affect my car loan EMI?
Your EMI is affected by the loan amount, interest rate, loan tenure, and your down payment. A larger down payment reduces your loan amount and subsequently your EMI.
