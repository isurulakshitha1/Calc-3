Car Loan Eligibility Checklist: Are You Ready to Apply?

Thinking of applying for a car loan? Before you do, make sure you meet the key eligibility criteria. Here's a simple checklist to help you prepare and boost your approval chances.

1. Age Requirement

Most banks require applicants to be at least 21 years old and not older than 60 at loan maturity.

2. Employment Status

You must have a stable job or business. Salaried employees should have at least 1 year of work experience, and self-employed individuals usually need 2–3 years of business proof.

3. Income Level

The minimum monthly income is typically ₹15,000–₹20,000 (or equivalent in your country). The higher your income, the higher the loan you can get.

4. Credit Score

A credit score of 700+ is ideal. Check your score beforehand and correct any errors. Lower scores may still qualify but with higher interest rates.

5. Debt-to-Income Ratio

Lenders check how much of your income goes to paying existing debts. A lower ratio (under 40%) improves your chances of approval.

6. Documents Checklist

  • Proof of identity (Aadhaar/passport/license)
  • Proof of address (utility bill/ration card)
  • Income proof (payslips, ITRs)
  • Bank statements (last 3–6 months)

7. Down Payment Preparedness

Having a decent down payment (15–20%) not only boosts approval but also lowers EMI and total interest.

8. Loan Tenure Preference

Be clear about how long you want to repay the loan. Shorter tenure = lower interest but higher EMI. Use our EMI Calculator to test what works best for you.

Final Tip

If you don’t meet one or two criteria, don’t panic. Some banks offer joint loans (with a spouse or parent), or may adjust interest rate. Talk to your lender before giving up.

FAQs

Can I get a car loan without income proof?
Some lenders offer loans against collateral or co-applicant income, but it’s harder. Proof improves approval.
Is PAN card mandatory?
In India, yes. In other countries, equivalent national ID or tax numbers are required.
